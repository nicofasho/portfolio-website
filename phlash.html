Project Overview

Phlash is a flash card app that allows users to easily create flash card decks for any subject that they want to learn.

Competitive Research

This project began with competitive research, where I looked at 3 other flash card apps to see how competitors approached the problem of providing a way for users to create and use flashcards.

User Research

The next step for this project was User Research in which I interviewed 3 participants that would potentially be users of the finished app. This was done to gain insights into the needs and wants of users so that I could better design the app.

After the 3 interviews I broke down the long form answers into statements where the participants were doing, thinking, or feeling something. This will help me always keep the users in mind when designing features in the future. You can download the full report here

User Persona

I created one persona for this project. Her name is Melanie, and she is a young go-getter who is constantly trying to improve her skills and knowledge.

Here are some of her goals / needs:

  • To make flashcards to learn work-specific terms.
  • To be able to prioritize some concepts over others.
  • A way to assess her mastery of the material she's studying.
  • Some sort of reward for correct answers, gamification.
  • To become more competent at her job.

Melanie's problem statement is: Melanie needs a way to study flashcards that she wants to make, because she wants to master work-specific terms and acronyms.

Using this information, I came up with a Hypothesis Statement: We believe that by creating a flashcard / test app for Melanie, we will achieve having Melanie contribute 50% more in her meetings at work.

Information Architecture

Next I conducted Task Analyses on the two most important tasks for Melanie, which were: Making a flashcard deck and testing against an existing deck. Here is the task analysis for making a flashcard deck:

Entry Point: Open the app

Success Criteria: Flashcard deck finished

  • Tap 'Create Flashcard Deck' button
  • Write the concept/question on the front side of the card
  • Tap 'done' button
  • Write the answer/definition on the back side of the card
  • Check both sides of the card
  • Tap 'Done' button
  • Repeat with front and back sides of cards until desired amount is reached
  • Tap 'Finish Deck' button
  • Success criteria: Flashcard Deck Finished

Using the information from the Task Analyses I drew a Task Flow for each task, which you can find in the zip file along with the other task analysis here

Usability Testing

After generating the simple prototype I tested it with 3 participants who would potentially use the app. I created the script and 4 scenario tasks.

Scenario Tasks:

  1. You just got home from your first day at your training course. You want to log in to Phlash.
  2. You learned a lot of new terms so you want to create and name a new deck.
  3. After naming the deck you decide to make a new card in the deck.
  4. You have created a deck for your first day of your training course. Now you want to test your knowledge against the deck.
